The Justification

Demographics &
Market Resilience

Jerantut currently faces a significant housing deficit amid strong owner-occupier demand. Concurrently, the district has entered an aging stage, alongside a noticeable youth out-migration to urban centers. The NADI masterplan directly addresses this structural gap by focusing on three core pillars.

01
Resilient Retirement
Building a resilient retirement ecosystem through barrier-free assisted living, geriatric healthcare, and biophilic social spaces.
02
Premium Wellness
Positioning Jerantut as a premium wellness destination by integrating advanced post-operative care with local eco-tourism assets.
03
Youth Retention
Reducing youth outmigration through the introduction of medical and biotech industries, high-value jobs, and flexible housing incentives.

The Masterplan

A comprehensive 1,110.64-acre layout meticulously balanced with 51% sellable and 49% non-sellable land allocation to prioritize ecological harmony.

Universal Housing

Weaving youth migration and elderly aging into a unified ecosystem with accessible ground-levels and flexible upper-level youth studios.

  • Taman Degupan & Serenia: 20'x70' Terrace

Strategic Rollout

Implementation Strategy

The development adopts a thematic and zoning approach with "Transit-First" logic, catalyzing early economic momentum before expanding into specialized healthcare tourism.

Degupan NADI

Youth & Transit Zone. Prioritizes Transit-Oriented Development (TOD) at the KTM station to catalyze early regulatory momentum and establish a trained workforce pipeline through the local university. This phase stabilizes the foundational economy of the township.

Bayu NADI

Tourism & Commerce Zone. Activates tourism anchors including the MICE Center, hotels, and the Inovasi industrial parks to drive visitor engagement and regional economic activity. This expands Jerantut's footprint as an eco-medical destination.

Hayat NADI

Care & Wellness Zone. Completes the township with the geriatric care hospital and specialized health facilities. By this stage, the township has a mature, local workforce capable of supporting a premier, self-sufficient retirement destination.
